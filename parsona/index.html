2.4 Where Your Data is Stored

  • All profile data is stored in private GitHub gists (GitHub, Inc., USA).
  • GitHub acts as our data processor. GitHub complies with GDPR through Standard Contractual Clauses.
  • Your password is never stored in plain text—only a cryptographic hash (SHA-256).
  • Private data fields are encrypted with AES-256 encryption using your password as the key.

2.5 Public vs. Private Data

  • Public Data: Your profile information (name, description, images, social links, etc.) is intentionally displayed publicly so others can view your profile. This is the core purpose of the service.
  • Private Data: Any data marked as private is encrypted with your password. We cannot read your encrypted private data—mathematically impossible without your password.

2.6 We CANNOT Read Your Private Data

⚠️ Zero-Knowledge Encryption:
Your password is used to encrypt private data on YOUR device (in your browser) before it's ever sent to our servers. We never see your password or unencrypted private data. Even if we wanted to read your private data, we mathematically cannot—we don't have the decryption key (your password). This is called "zero-knowledge encryption" and means your private data is truly private.

2.6a What We Can and Cannot See

What we CAN see (public data):

  • Profile name, description, country, gender, etc. (everything displayed on your public profile)
  • Image URLs (the links you provide, not the images themselves)
  • Social media links
  • Password hash (one-way encrypted, cannot be reversed)

What we CANNOT see (encrypted data):

  • Your actual password
  • Any data marked as "private" in your profile
  • The unencrypted content of encrypted fields

We do NOT:

  • Monitor or read your profile content (we may only see it if viewing your public profile like any other user)
  • Analyze, mine, or process your content for any purpose other than displaying it
  • Use your data for advertising, marketing, or profiling
  • Share your data with third parties (except GitHub as our hosting provider)
  • Track your activity or use cookies/analytics
